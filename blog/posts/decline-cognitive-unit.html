The Decline of the Cognitive Unit

04/03/2026 Data Science Philosophy

Life as a student in 2026

I sit here in my apartment looking over the vast landscape of San Diego. I guess its beautiful. I'm thinking to myself, where did it all go wrong? I'm a smart guy. I just wanted to write code, do math, solve difficult problems, and maybe buy my mom a house at the end of it all. And now look at me. 5 years of work, and I'm not even close to a "junior developer." Defined by a number called grade point average, which in reality means nothing. It wont get you a job, only access to more schooling. 3.9. Okay but what do you know? Well I don't know shit! Here I am preparing to enter the work force, at one of the absolute worst times in recent history. What the fuck am I supposed to do. I don't have a single skill that a computer can't do besides the ones given to me by my hands and legs. I can cut wood, hunt, lay brick, lift heavy weight, and run far. I am an animal. A vessel of muscle and blood with the sole purpose of ensuring the preservation of my genetic code. A young fellow like me simply wants to play golf with the lads, rip 360 no scopes on copious stimulants, and generally generate dopamine through the accumulation of novel experiences accompanied by like minded individuals while having my basic primal needs met. And here the fuck I am competing with a damn robot instead. The value of the cognitive unit has significantly declined in this new age. Im fighting inflation on multiple axes now. The US dollar, the cognitive unit, housing, food, wealth inequality, and corporate greed. I cannot simply live comfortably and easily just because I am smarter than the average fry. And if its not a damn robot I'm competing with, its a senior developer with 15 years of industry experience and a masters degree who just got layed off from Google after getting layed off from Oracle. Maybe AI has hit its limit, and this is where it ends. No AGI, no mega agents. That would almost be nice. But I think I'd rather just see it to its end. Maybe I'll be pleasantly surprised.

Planets and Intelligence

In the mean time, I still have another 8 months of school, and another x years of "seeing this to its end." I just want to make a living and have fun doing it. Do something good with myself. Make sure my family is taken care of. Contribute to humanity. A dog would be fun too, I like golden retrievers. Im tired of all this animal maxxing shit we do as humans. Always concerning ourselves with the meaningless meanderings of the many. I feel like humans are to intelligence as Venus is to the Sun. Earth is perfectly nudged in that sweet spot for equilibrium and life. While Venus has to sit and watch from afar, wishing its orbit was closer to that of Earths. Instead of being in that intellectual sweet spot, we are stuck in the strange intermediate band of intellect that is not enough, yet too much at the same time. Like either be smart or dont at all. One step above monkeys ain't cutting it for me. We're too dumb to exist optimally, and smart enough to understand how suboptimal our existence is. The human race is like the human centipede. We're all just eating each others own shit because we were programmed to. Wheres my fucking red pill at! Ive never seen human centipede or matrix. Regardless, Im eating everyones shit and shitting it back out.

Monkeys

Do you know monkeys peel bananas from the bottom of the banana? Or is it the top? This must mean that I should peel the banana from the bottom too. Monkeys know what they're doing, they've been peeling bananas forever. Why the fuck would a monkey know how to peel a banana better than me. Last time I peeled a banana like that I got banana all over my hand. Peeling it from the stem is much better because on rare occasion it will rip off clean all at once leaving a nice cup shape near the stem, connected to the shaft by one edge of the banana. That is good. That is how you peel a banana.

Closing Thoughts

Anyways fuck all this rat race shit Im just tryna chill, vibe, and perhaps procreate, not fight against superhuman intelligence to make a living. I loved coding, and now it is gone. A lost art. The passion I used to feel and value I used to have is now replaced by Claude API tokens and a prompt.
